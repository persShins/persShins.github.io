Masking Binarized Neural Networks Side-Channel Resistance

Abstract / Summary

This paper studies first-order masking for neural-network components that are difficult to protect against side-channel leakage, especially activation and ArgMax gadgets. The work targets side-channel resistant neural network implementations and provides practical masked building blocks for secure inference.

Key Points

  • Designs first-order masked activation and ArgMax gadgets.
  • Focuses on side-channel resistant neural network implementations.
  • Provides implementation-oriented countermeasures for cryptographic and embedded-security settings.

Public Coefficient Matters: A Practical Differential Fault Attack on ML-DSA and HAETAE
WonGeun Shin, SeungHyeon Jeon, Daehyeon Bae, Sujin Park, HeeSeok Kim
IACR Cryptology ePrint Archive, Report 2026/1344.
A practical differential fault attack on ML-DSA and HAETAE, showing how public coefficients can affect key recovery in post-quantum signature implementations.
Paper
Fault Attack ML-DSA HAETAE Post-Quantum Cryptography

Abstract / Summary

This paper presents a practical differential fault attack against ML-DSA and HAETAE. It focuses on the role of public coefficients in lattice-based signature schemes and shows how faulty signatures can be used for key recovery.

Key Points

  • Studies practical differential fault attacks on post-quantum digital signatures.
  • Targets ML-DSA and HAETAE implementations.
  • Highlights the role of public coefficients in fault-based key recovery.
